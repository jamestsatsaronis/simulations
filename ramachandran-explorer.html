The Ramachandran principle

Rotate the two backbone bonds either side of the central alanine's Cα (the φ and ψ angles) and watch when the atoms collide. Most φ/ψ combinations force non-bonded atoms to overlap — those are physically impossible. The rare clash-free combinations are exactly the ones that make α-helices and β-strands.
