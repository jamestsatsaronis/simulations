Amino acid side chain classification

Each amino acid is shown by its side chain (R group); the backbone is drawn in grey so your attention stays on the R group. Select every category that applies — many side chains fit more than one — then choose Check answer for a tag-by-tag breakdown and the structural reason behind each tag.

Every structure is drawn as it exists at pH 7, as a zwitterion: the backbone α-amino group is protonated (–NH₃⁺, or –NH₂⁺ in proline) and the α-carboxyl group is deprotonated (–COO⁻). Ionisable side chains follow the same rule — aspartate and glutamate as carboxylates (–COO⁻), lysine as –NH₃⁺, and arginine's guanidinium group protonated (+). Histidine (side-chain pKa ≈ 6) is shown neutral, because at pH 7 it is only partly protonated.
