β-sheet · antiparallel

Antiparallel inter-strand H-bonds

The N-terminal β-hairpin: two strands running in opposite directions. Backbone only, ball-and-stick, coloured by atom; green dashed lines are the inter-strand N–H···O=C hydrogen bonds. In antiparallel sheets they sit nearly straight across, in alternating narrow and wide pairs.

β-sheet · parallel

Parallel inter-strand H-bonds

Two strands running the same direction (β1 and β5 of ubiquitin's mixed sheet). Backbone only, ball-and-stick, coloured by atom; here the green dashed hydrogen bonds run at a slant and are evenly spaced — the tell-tale contrast with the antiparallel pattern beside it.

β-sheet · amphipathic

Side-chain orientation & sheet faces

The full four-stranded sheet with side chains in ball-and-stick, coloured by hydrophobicity (hydrophobic green, the rest grey). Because side chains alternate above and below the sheet, one face is hydrophobic — it packs against the α-helix in the core — and the other is polar. Rotate to see the two faces.
